## Core Features

### 1. Earnings DNA — The Personal Risk Engine

When a worker onboards, InsureFlow's ML model analyses their last 10–12 weeks of earnings data to build a personal baseline. This is not a generic average — it's specific to their zones, their work days, their hours, and their historical performance patterns.

This Earnings DNA powers three things:
- The Protection Floor (75% of personal baseline = guaranteed minimum)
- The weekly premium calculation (personalised, not one-size-fits-all)
- Fraud detection (any claim that doesn't match the worker's own behaviour pattern is flagged)

```
Earnings DNA Example — Raju

Mon–Fri avg:  ₹850–920/day
Saturday avg: ₹1,040/day  (weekend surge)
Sunday avg:   ₹640/day    (typically works half day)

Weekly baseline: ₹5,800
Protection Floor (75%): ₹4,350
Extended Floor (85%): ₹4,930  [+₹18/week upgrade]
```

### 2. AI Zone Intelligence Map — Red / Amber / Green / Surge

The AI builds a live risk heatmap of the city every hour by fusing:
- IMD real-time rainfall API
- CPCB AQI feed
- Historical zone-level waterlogging and flood data
- Anonymized GPS activity of all active InsureFlow workers
- Zomato order density patterns (mocked for demo)

**Zone Classifications:**

🔴 **Red Zone** — AQI >300 or rainfall >65mm/hr. Trigger likely active. Coverage fires automatically. Worker advised to halt.

🟡 **Amber Zone** — AQI 200–300 or moderate congestion. Early warning issued. Worker advised to finish current batch and reassess in 45 min.

🟢 **Green Zone** — Disruption rate <10%. Lowest premium tier. Ideal earning window.

🔵 **Surge Zone** — Post-disruption demand spike predicted in 60 min. High earnings opportunity. Workers nudged proactively toward this zone.

> When BTM Layout goes red and Indiranagar goes blue, InsureFlow tells Raju before he wastes 2 hours waiting for orders that will never come.

### 3. Five Parametric Triggers

InsureFlow uses five precisely-defined triggers — not vague thresholds, but calibrated conditions that correspond to real, verifiable income loss events for food delivery workers.

| # | Trigger | Type | Threshold |
|---|---------|------|-----------|
| 1 | Heavy Rainfall | Environmental | IMD ≥65mm in 3hr window in active zone |
| 2 | Extreme Air Quality | Environmental | AQI ≥300 sustained ≥4 consecutive hours |
| 3 | Extreme Heat Advisory | Environmental | IMD heat alert + temp ≥44°C for ≥3hr peak |
| 4 | Local Curfew / Bandh | Social | Govt alert API OR ≥70% zone workers inactive simultaneously |
| 5 | Earnings Velocity Crash | Platform | 2hr earnings <40% of personal baseline + one confirmed external signal |

**Why Trigger 5 matters:** Triggers 1–4 rely on official data sources. But hyperlocal disruptions — a sudden road closure, a local protest, an area-specific water-logging event — may not appear in any API for hours. Trigger 5 uses the workers themselves as sensors. If Raju's earnings are tanking and 60% of nearby workers show the same pattern, that IS a disruption signal — regardless of whether the weather API has caught up yet.

### 4. Smart Claim Pre-Validation — Zero Touch, Full Trust

The moment a trigger condition is detected, InsureFlow starts pre-validating the claim in the background. By the time the trigger officially fires, the claim is already 80–90% complete.

### 5. Fraud Detection — Six Layers, Zero Confrontation

InsureFlow never accuses a worker. It simply cross-validates. Fraud is caught by data, not interrogation.

**Layer 1 — GPS Spoofing Detection**
Worker claims disruption but GPS places them at home during the event. Movement pattern analysis also catches teleportation-style GPS jumps — the signature of a VPN/mock location app.
`Signal: GPS coordinates vs zone boundary + movement velocity`

**Layer 2 — Earnings DNA Anomaly**
Worker never works Thursdays historically but files a claim for a Thursday disruption. The personal baseline model flags behavioural inconsistencies silently.
`Signal: Claim day/time vs 12-week historical work pattern`

**Layer 3 — Duplicate & Coordinated Claims**
Multiple accounts from the same device, IP, or household filing claims on the same event. Cluster detection catches family-ring fraud and organised schemes.
`Signal: Device fingerprint + IP + submission timing cluster analysis`

**Layer 4 — Weather Data Mismatch**
Worker claims heavy rain but IMD data shows clear skies in their specific zone at that time. Claims are always validated against raw API data — never self-reported information.
`Signal: IMD/AQI raw data vs claimed disruption type and timestamp`

**Layer 5 — Velocity Fraud**
Filing claims every week regardless of conditions is statistically anomalous. Claims-per-month is tracked against zone disruption frequency. Outliers are auto-flagged.
`Signal: Claim frequency vs zone disruption rate over rolling 8-week window`

**Layer 6 — Peer Network Divergence**
If 90% of workers in the same zone continued earning normally during the period one worker is claiming disruption, that isolated claim is flagged. You cannot fake a disruption that left everyone around you unaffected.
`Signal: Claimant inactivity vs zone-wide worker activity for same window`

Premium is calculated fresh every Monday morning. It is never hidden, never confusing, always explained in plain language.

No jargon. No fine print. No surprises.

### 7. Bridge Advance — Because Sunday Is Too Late

When a disruption is detected mid-week and the earnings velocity monitor shows Raju is significantly below his daily target, InsureFlow doesn't wait for the Sunday settlement.

It sends a **Bridge Advance** to his UPI immediately — a partial payout that keeps him financially stable through the rest of the week. This is settled against the final Sunday claim automatically.

Rent is due on the 1st. Fuel is needed today. InsureFlow understands both.

### 8. Explainable AI Panel

Every premium change and every claim trigger is explained in one plain sentence — in Hindi or English, whichever the worker prefers.

- *"Your premium went up ₹8 this week because of the cyclone warning near coastal Karnataka."*
- *"Your claim triggered because IMD confirmed 78mm rainfall in BTM Layout between 11 AM and 2 PM, and your GPS confirms you were in the zone."*
- *"Your claim was not triggered because your GPS showed you were inactive at home before the rain started."*

No black box. No confusion. Full trust.

## Technical Architecture

### Platform Choice — Mobile-First PWA

Raju uses a Redmi 9A. He has 2GB RAM and fluctuating mobile data. InsureFlow is built as a **Progressive Web App (PWA)** — it works on any Android browser, installs to the home screen like a native app, functions with partial offline support, and doesn't require a Play Store download or a 100MB install.

This is not a design choice. It is a Raju choice.

### Tech Stack

```
Frontend         React 18 + Tailwind CSS (PWA, mobile-first)
Backend          Node.js + Express.js
Database         PostgreSQL (worker profiles, claims, earnings history)
ML / AI          Python — scikit-learn (premium model, anomaly detection)
                 TensorFlow (earnings velocity forecasting)
Real-time        WebSockets for live zone map updates
Push Alerts      Firebase Cloud Messaging
Payments         Razorpay test mode (simulated UPI payouts)

APIs (Live)
  Weather        IMD API / OpenWeatherMap
  Air Quality    CPCB AQI API
  Maps/Zones     Google Maps Geocoding API

APIs (Mocked for Demo)
  Platform data  Zomato order density (synthetic)
  GPS activity   Worker location feed (synthetic)
  Govt alerts    Curfew/bandh notification API (synthetic)
```

### ML Models

**Weekly Premium Calculator**
Input features: zone risk score (7-day rolling), worker's historical claim rate, IMD 7-day forecast confidence, personal earnings variance index.
Model: Gradient Boosted Regressor trained on synthetic but realistic gig worker earnings + disruption data.
Output: Personalised weekly premium (₹35–₹80 range).

**Earnings Velocity Forecaster**
Input features: historical earnings by day/hour, zone, weather conditions, day of week, festive calendar.
Model: LSTM time-series forecaster.
Output: Expected earnings at each hour of a shift. Used for real-time deviation detection.

**Anomaly / Fraud Detector**
Input features: GPS trace, claim frequency, earnings pattern, peer activity divergence score, device fingerprint hash.
Model: Isolation Forest for unsupervised anomaly detection.
Output: Fraud risk score (0–1). Above 0.7 = manual review. Above 0.9 = auto-rejection.

## Business Model

InsureFlow operates on a **weekly subscription premium** paid every Monday. Workers pay only for weeks they want coverage — no annual lock-in, no exit penalty. This matches how gig workers actually think about money: week by week.

**Unit Economics (Illustrative)**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Average weekly premium per worker | ₹52 |
| Average weekly payout per worker | ₹180 (disruption weeks only) |
| Disruption weeks per worker per year | ~8–10 weeks |
| Annual premium collected per worker | ₹2,704 |
| Annual expected payout per worker | ₹1,440–₹1,800 |
| Loss ratio (target) | 55–65% |

**Fraud detection target:** Reduce fraudulent payouts to <3% of total claims volume. Each fraud detection layer is estimated to eliminate a specific fraud vector — combined, they protect 12–18% of gross written premium.

**Distribution:** InsureFlow integrates directly into the Zomato partner app as an opt-in toggle. No separate app download. No separate sign-up. The onboarding friction is near-zero because the trust infrastructure (Zomato KYC, earnings history, GPS data) already exists.
